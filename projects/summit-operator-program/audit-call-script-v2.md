# Summit AIS — Audit Call Framework
# 60-Minute Diagnostic Call — Home Service ICP ($500K–$5M)

**Use case:** Prospect booked a 60-min audit call after outbound or ad. They've seen the short video. They're curious, not sold. Your job is to diagnose their business across 3 pillars, quantify the leak, show the architecture of the fix, and book the Blueprint call within 48 hours.

**You are NOT pitching on this call.** You are the founder who built a $300K season out of the same mess they're in. The audit sells the blueprint. The blueprint sells the close.

---

## Pre-Call Prep (15 minutes, done the night before or morning of)

Block 15 min before the call. Do the homework. Prospects feel it when you've done it and when you haven't.

### 1. GHL — Pull the contact record (2 min)
- Lead source (ad / referral / Student Works operator / cold call)
- Form/Typeform answers — revenue range, team size, service type, biggest problem
- Tags applied, any previous touchpoints
- **Flag:** anything they wrote in the "what's your biggest problem" field — that's your opening thread

### 2. Their Website (3 min)
- Does it exist? How old does it look?
- Can you get a quote online? How many clicks from homepage → booked?
- Is there a phone number above the fold?
- Any reviews/testimonials embedded?
- **Score silently:** 0-10. Below 6 = sales ops pillar is wide open.

### 3. Google Business Profile + Maps (3 min)
- Search "[service] + [their city]" — where do they rank in the map pack?
- Their GBP: photos count, review count, average star rating, last review date
- Do they respond to reviews?
- Categories set correctly? Service areas listed?
- **Flag:** if they have <30 reviews OR reviews are >60 days old, the review system is broken

### 4. Social Media (3 min)
- Instagram: post frequency, follower count, are they posting job photos?
- Facebook: same check
- TikTok / YouTube: rare for this ICP but check if they mentioned it
- **What you're looking for:** are they generating brand or just existing?

### 5. Review Sites (2 min)
- BBB rating and any unresolved complaints
- HomeStars / HomeAdvisor / Angi if Canada/US
- Yelp if US
- **Flag:** any 1-star pattern — ask about it gently on the call

### 6. Competitor glance (2 min)
- Search their service + city, pick top 3 ranked competitors
- Compare: more reviews? Better website? Online booking?
- **Purpose:** you'll name a competitor mid-call. "Your closest competitor X has 4x your review count — that's $Y in search traffic they're getting that you're not."

## The 60-Minute Call Structure

| Block | Minutes | Goal |
|-------|---------|------|
| 1. Opening + Frame | 0–5 | Set the tone. Earn the right to ask hard questions. |
| 2. Discovery — Sales Ops | 5–20 | Diagnose how leads become cash |
| 3. Discovery — Business Ops | 20–35 | Diagnose how jobs get done |
| 4. Discovery — Team Ops | 35–45 | Diagnose how people get hired, trained, managed |
| 5. Pain Agitation + Cost of Inaction | 45–50 | Quantify what it's costing them to stay here |
| 6. Diagnosis | 50–55 | Reflect back the 3 biggest leaks in their words |
| 7. Solution Overview (Summit 3 Pillars) | 55–62 | Show the architecture. Not the pitch. |
| 8. Pricing + Guarantee | 62–67 | Drop the number. Short. |
| 9. Objection Block | as needed | Handle the real objection in one question |
| 10. Close to Blueprint | 67–70 | Two specific slots. Done. |

If they're tight on time and you have to cut, drop Pillar 3 (Team Ops) first, then shorten the Solution Overview. Never cut Discovery or Diagnosis — those do the selling.

## BLOCK 1 — Opening + Frame (5 min)

### The Opening (exact lines)

> "Hey [Name], appreciate you making the time. Before we jump in — how's your day going so far?"

Let them answer. 30 seconds of human before business.

> "Cool. So here's how I want to run this. I've got about an hour blocked. I'm going to ask you a bunch of questions about how [their company] actually runs right now — sales, operations, team. Not because I'm being nosy. Because until I understand where your leaks are, I can't tell you if we can help. By the end of the hour, you'll have a clear picture of where the revenue is hiding in your business, and I'll tell you straight up whether we're a fit or not. Fair?"

**Wait for "fair" or "yeah sounds good."** That's your verbal contract to dig in.

> "Before I start asking questions — what made you book this call? What's the thing driving you crazy right now?"

**Shut up. Let them talk.** Take literal notes — exact phrases. They'll come back in Block 6.

If they give a short answer, one follow-up:
> "Say more about that. When's the last time that cost you a job?"

## BLOCK 2 — Discovery: Sales Operations (15 min)

### Frame
> "Alright let's start at the top. I want to walk through how a lead turns into money in your business. From the first time someone raises their hand, all the way to them paying you."

### Questions (pick 6-8, prioritize based on their answers)

1. **"Where are your leads coming from right now? Walk me through the sources."**
   - Reveals: channel mix, dependency on one source, paid vs organic
   - Listen for: "word of mouth" = no system. "Google" = check their rank. "Ads" = ask who runs them.

2. **"When a new lead comes in at 7pm on a Tuesday — what actually happens?"**
   - Reveals: speed-to-lead, who owns response, after-hours gap
   - Listen for: "I get to it in the morning" = 50%+ leak. "My wife handles it" = single point of failure.

3. **"How fast are you responding on average? Minutes? Hours? Next day?"**
   - If >5 min → they're losing half their leads and don't know it
   - Flag: "The data on this is brutal — a 5-minute response vs a 30-minute response is the difference between an 80% and 20% contact rate."

4. **"After the first contact — what's the follow-up sequence look like?"**
   - Reveals: whether follow-up exists at all
   - 95% answer: "I follow up once and if they don't get back, I move on."
   - That's a $20-50K/season leak.

5. **"Out of every 10 leads, how many actually turn into paying jobs?"**
   - Don't accept "I don't know" — push: "Ballpark. 2? 5? 8?"
   - Close rate diagnosis:
     - <30% = sales process broken
     - 30-50% = normal, lots of room
     - 50-70% = good, optimize for volume
     - 70%+ = they're cherry-picking, need more top-of-funnel

6. **"What CRM are you using?"**
   - Answers you'll hear: "Jobber" / "HCP" / "spreadsheet" / "my head" / "phone + notes app"
   - No CRM = revenue leaking everywhere and no visibility on it

7. **"How does someone book a job with you? Do they have to call, or can they book online?"**
   - No online booking = they're losing every buyer who doesn't want to call
   - Especially critical for <$500 ticket services (window cleaning, gutter)

8. **"Last month — how many quotes did you send out, and how many closed?"**
   - Forces a real number. If they can't answer → no pipeline tracking.

9. **"What's your average job value?"**
   - Need this for the cost-of-inaction math in Block 5.

10. **"Are you tracking which lead source converts best?"**
    - Almost always no. Which means they're spending ad money blind.

### What You're Diagnosing

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Response time >5 min | Losing 40-60% of leads to competitors |
| "Follow up once then move on" | Missing 50% of winnable deals (5-7 touches is the data) |
| No CRM | Can't tell you close rate, source attribution, or pipeline value |
| No online booking | Losing the 30% of buyers who won't call |
| Owner is the one responding | Scales to $500K then breaks |
| Close rate <30% | Pitch/qualifying problem, not a lead problem |
| No source tracking | Spending ad dollars blind |

### Plant the Seed (mid-block, once you have 3+ signals)

> "So let me play back what I'm hearing. You've got leads coming in from [X sources]. When one comes in, [Y person] responds in [Z hours]. You follow up [once/not at all]. You don't know which source converts best. That's not a you problem — that's what 90% of home service businesses look like at your size. It also means every dollar of ad spend is doing half of what it could, and every busy week you have — and you're about to have a lot of them — leads are falling through the cracks faster than you can catch them."

Don't pitch. Just name it and move on.

## BLOCK 3 — Discovery: Business Operations (15 min)

### Frame
> "Cool. Second pillar — now let's talk about what happens after someone's booked. Job scheduled, money still theoretical. Walk me through it."

### Questions (pick 6-8)

1. **"Once a job's booked — how does your crew know where to go, what to do, and what to bring?"**
   - Reveals: scheduling + dispatch system, job details handoff
   - Listen for: "group text" / "I text them each morning" = chaos at 10+ jobs/day

2. **"What tool are you using to schedule jobs?"**
   - Paper / calendar / Jobber / spreadsheet / "in my head"
   - Anything that isn't a proper tool = bottleneck

3. **"Walk me through a typical day — how does your morning start?"**
   - Reveals: how much the owner runs logistics vs leads
   - If they spend >2hrs on dispatch and prep, that's time not closing deals

4. **"When a job gets done — how does the customer pay?"**
   - Credit card on site? Invoice after? E-transfer?
   - Listen for: "I invoice them and chase for 2 weeks" = cash flow leak

5. **"How long does it take you on average to get paid after a job's done?"**
   - >7 days = collection problem
   - >30 days = severe cash flow drag

6. **"Do you know your profit margin per job type?"**
   - Almost always no. Which means they might be losing money on certain services and not know it.

7. **"What happens if a crew doesn't show up or a job runs long?"**
   - Tests their exception handling. Reveals how fragile the system is.

8. **"How do you request reviews after a job?"**
   - "I ask them" / "Sometimes" / "Automated text" / "Don't really"
   - This is free money being left on the table

9. **"How often do past customers rebook? Do you have a recurring/membership model?"**
   - No recurring revenue = starting from zero every season
   - Huge lever — memberships can be 40% of revenue for mature operators

10. **"What's your Google review count, and what was the last one?"**
    - If you already checked pre-call, reference it: "I saw you had 43 reviews, last one was 2 months ago. What's happening there?"

### What You're Diagnosing

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Manual dispatch via text | Breaks at 10+ jobs/day, owner-bottleneck |
| No payment-on-site | 7-30 day AR delay, working capital stuck |
| Don't know per-job margin | Might be losing money on some services |
| No review automation | Leaving 10-30 reviews/month on the table |
| No membership/recurring | Rebuilding revenue from zero every season |
| Owner does dispatch | Can't step out of ops, can't sell while running ops |

### Plant the Seed

> "So the picture I'm getting — you're the dispatcher, you're the salesperson, you're the quality control, and you're the collections department. That works at [current revenue]. It doesn't work at [2x revenue]. The question isn't whether it breaks — it's when, and how bad it is when it does."

## BLOCK 4 — Discovery: Team Operations (10 min)

### Frame
> "Last pillar — the team. How you hire, how you train, how you keep people."

### Questions (pick 5-7)

1. **"How many people do you have right now — crew plus office?"**
2. **"Walk me through how you hired your last person. What did that process look like?"**
   - Listen for: "kid of a friend" / "Indeed" / "I don't really have a process"

3. **"When a new person starts, how do you train them?"**
   - "They ride with me for a week" = owner-dependent
   - "We have a video/doc" = rare, big green flag

4. **"Do you know who your best and worst crew members are? Measurably?"**
   - Reveals: performance tracking
   - Almost always "I have a feeling" not "I have a number"

5. **"If you needed to hire 3 people next week for the season — could you? Where would you go?"**
   - Most answers: "I'd post on Indeed and pray"
   - That's a crisis, not a system

6. **"What's your turnover look like year over year?"**
   - Home service averages 40-60% — if they're above that, culture/pay/training is broken

7. **"Are your techs selling on site? Upsells, memberships, add-ons?"**
   - Rare, massive lever. A crew that sells is 2-3x the lifetime value.

### What You're Diagnosing

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| No hiring pipeline | Scrambling every season, reactive hiring = bad hires |
| No documented training | Quality drops with each new person, owner is the trainer |
| "I ride with them" training | Doesn't scale past 3-4 crew |
| No performance tracking | Can't identify or fix weak links |
| Crews don't sell on site | Leaving 20-30% revenue per job on table |
| Turnover >60% | Culture / pay / systems problem |

### Plant the Seed

> "You're walking into the busiest 6 months of the year. If hiring and training don't have a system, you'll spend the season putting out fires — replacing the guy who quit in June, training the new guy in July, losing the customer he messed up in August. That's the cycle most home service owners are in. That's not because you're bad at this — it's because the system doesn't exist yet."

## BLOCK 5 — Pain Agitation + Cost of Inaction (5 min)

### Frame
> "Alright, give me one second — I want to do some quick math with you based on what you just told me."

### Pick the ROI Frame That Hits Hardest

Based on what they said, pick ONE. Don't do all three — it'll feel gimmicky.

#### Frame A — The Lost Job Calculation (best when they described a follow-up gap)

Use their actual numbers.

> "You said you get about [X] leads a month and close [Y]. So you're losing [X-Y] leads. Even if half of those weren't serious buyers — that's [half] winnable jobs you're losing every month. Your average job is [$Z]. That's [half × Z] a month in missed revenue. Over a 6-month season, that's [6 × half × Z]. And that's just from the follow-up leak — we haven't touched lead speed, conversion rate, or reactivation."

Let the number sit. Don't fill the silence.

#### Frame B — The Owner Time Cost (best when they're clearly the bottleneck)

> "Walk me through this — you said you spend about [X hours/week] on [dispatch / follow-up / quoting / training]. At your revenue of [$Y] a year, your effective rate is roughly [$Y/2000] an hour. That means you're spending [X × rate × 52] a year on work that shouldn't be on your plate. That's before we even talk about what you could be doing with those hours — closing deals, growing, going home at 6pm."

#### Frame C — The Churn Cost (best when they have no recurring revenue or bad reviews)

> "You've got [X] past customers and no rebooking system. Industry data on home service says about 40% of customers will rebook if asked, 15% if you just wait. That gap — 25% of [X] — is [X × 0.25] jobs you could be running this season from customers you already paid to acquire. At [$Z] a job, that's [X × 0.25 × Z] in pure profit revenue, because acquisition is already paid. That's before any of the new stuff we'd do."

### The Close of Block 5

> "Here's the thing — you don't have a demand problem. You don't have a pricing problem. You have a **system** problem. Every month that system stays broken, it costs you [number you just said]. And it compounds, because every unhappy customer, every lost lead, every untrained hire — they all cascade. The question isn't whether you can afford to fix it. It's whether you can afford another 6 months of the leak."

Pause. Let it breathe.

## BLOCK 6 — Diagnosis (5 min)

### Frame
> "Let me play back what I heard. Tell me if I'm wrong on any of this."

### The 3-Leak Framework

Pick the 3 biggest issues from the call. Use THEIR exact phrases.

**Leak 1 — Sales Ops:**
> "You told me [exact quote — e.g., 'I try to get back to leads same day but honestly sometimes it takes me till the next morning']. That's your biggest leak. Speed-to-lead under 5 minutes would probably double your contact rate. You're losing deals before they even know you exist."

**Leak 2 — Business Ops:**
> "You also said [quote — e.g., 'I'm the one dispatching every morning, yeah']. You ARE the system right now. That's normal at your size, but it's also the single biggest risk to hitting your next level. The day you can't work, the business stops."

**Leak 3 — Team Ops OR Missed Lever (whichever was worse):**
> "And when I asked about [reviews / memberships / hiring pipeline / crew selling] — you said [quote]. That's the free money sitting on the floor. [Specific lever] alone is a 20-30% revenue uplift for businesses that install it right."

### The Bridge

> "None of this is you being bad at your business. Every home service owner at your size hits this same wall. The guys who break through have one thing in common — they stop being the system and start installing one. That's what we do."

## BLOCK 7 — Solution Overview: The Summit 3 Pillars (7 min)

### Frame
> "Let me show you what we actually install — not as a pitch, but so you understand the architecture. If it makes sense for your business, we'll talk about what it costs and next steps. If it doesn't, I'll tell you that too."

**You are showing a blueprint, not selling a package.** Talk like an architect describing a building.

### Pillar 1 — Sales Operations (2 min)

> "First pillar — Sales Operations. We install your CRM, your pipeline, your automated follow-up sequences, and your lead routing. When a lead comes in — whether from your website, Google, an ad, or a referral — it's captured automatically, routed to the right person, and followed up with. If they don't respond, it goes into a nurture sequence. If they book, the calendar updates everywhere at once. The goal: you never chase a lead again, and no lead falls through the cracks. Ever."

**Reference their pain:** "This directly fixes the [their quote about follow-up gap]."

### Pillar 2 — Business Operations (2 min)

> "Second pillar — Business Operations. We build you a new website that converts, set up your Google Business Profile, run your Meta ads for 2 months while we dial them in, install review automation, build reactivation campaigns for your past customers, and give you a Notion dashboard where you can see everything in one place — revenue, jobs, pipeline, crew performance. This is the front end of the business customers see and the back end you use to run it."

**Reference their pain:** "Remember you said [quote about reviews / website / past customers]? This is the part that fixes that."

### Pillar 3 — Team Operations (2 min)

> "Third pillar — Team. We document every SOP in your business, build you a training system new hires go through, and then we spend 2 months hands-on coaching you and your team on how to run everything. Not a PDF we send and disappear. We're in the trenches until your team can run it without us."

**Reference their pain:** "This is the piece that lets you step out of [dispatch / training / chasing]."

### The Close of Block 7

> "That's the whole stack. Built from what we did at TPS — $0 to $300K in 4.5 months using exactly this. Then we installed it in 8 other home service companies. Now we install it for you."

**Key principle:** Show the architecture. Don't oversell. Their imagination fills in the picture.

## BLOCK 8 — Pricing + Guarantee (3 min)

### Frame
Don't hide from pricing. Don't lead with it either. This goes AFTER they've seen the value.

> "Pricing — it's $9,000 to install everything I just walked through. That's a one-time fee. Two-month build, you keep everything we build forever, even if we never work together again after. No software subscriptions on top. No hidden fees. Nine grand, done."

### The Guarantee / Options

> "Two options on what happens after the 2-month build. Option A — takeover. You run it. Everything's documented, everything's yours. Option B — rev share. We stay on the backend, keep optimizing, and we take 20% of the revenue that comes through the systems we built. Which one makes sense depends on where your head's at after you see it running."

### The Anchor

> "For context — if you tried to build this separately, a decent website is 6-10K, a marketing agency is 3-5K a month, a CRM consultant is another 5-10K, and you'd still have to stitch it all together yourself. We do it for 9K flat. And most of our clients pay it back with 1-2 extra closed jobs in the first month."

### Brief Pause

> "That sit okay with you so far, or do you have questions on the number?"

## BLOCK 9 — Objection Handling (as they come)

Objections mean they're thinking about buying. Don't steamroll them. Handle the real one in one question.

### The 8 Core Objections

#### 1. "That's a lot of money" / "It's expensive"
> "It is. And if it didn't 10x return, it would be. Remember the [lost job / time cost] math we did? This pays itself back in [X jobs / Y weeks]. The real question isn't the 9K — it's whether you want this installed before the season hits or 6 months from now after you've lost the revenue you were going to spend it with."

#### 2. "I don't have time to build new systems right now"
> "That's exactly why this works. You don't build it — we do. You keep running the business. We install everything in the background and bring you in for what matters. The alternative is you keep doing it all yourself, which is why you're spinning. The 2 months we're installing is the only way you get OUT of that."

#### 3. "I can build this myself"
> "You could. Most founders technically can. The honest question is whether you WILL, and whether it'll be done before July. Building GHL proper, a converting website, ad campaigns, SOPs, review automation — that's 3 months of your time at minimum, and you're learning while you build. We do it in 3 weeks because we've done it 9 times. At your revenue, the opportunity cost of your 3 months is way more than 9K."

#### 4. "I got burned by an agency before"
> "I believe you. Most agencies I know took a retainer, sent screenshots of reports, and disappeared. We're structured completely different. One — you own everything we build. If you fire us tomorrow, you keep the CRM, the website, the ads, the docs. Two — rev share means we only eat if you eat. Three — we built this for our own home service company first. $300K in one season. Same system, same people you're talking to. We're not an agency renting you attention. We're operators installing infrastructure."

#### 5. "I need to think about it" / "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"
> "Totally fair. One question — what specifically do you need to think about? If it's the money, we can talk about it. If it's trust, we can talk about that. If it's timing, same. Whatever the real thing is — let's surface it now so you're not sitting on it for a week."
>
> If they insist: "Cool. When are you next talking to [partner]? Let's book the blueprint call for right after that. That way you both see the custom plan, and you can decide together."

#### 6. "Can you send me info first / a proposal / a deck?"
> "The blueprint IS your proposal. It's built specifically for your business, not a template. If I send you a generic PDF, you'll make up your mind based on a doc that doesn't actually apply to you. The blueprint call is 20 minutes, it's live, and you walk away with a custom plan whether we work together or not. That's way better than a deck."

#### 7. "My business isn't ready for this"
> "Tell me what 'ready' looks like. More revenue? More team? Slower season? Because here's the thing — every one of those gets easier AFTER you install systems, not before. Waiting until you're 'ready' is how you stay in the loop another 12 months. What specifically would need to be true for this to make sense right now?"

#### 8. "I'm already using [Jobber / HCP / HouseCall Pro]"
> "Great — those are good tools for what they do. They're scheduling and invoicing tools. What they don't do is your sales funnel, your ads, your reviews automation, your team SOPs, your follow-up engine, or your reactivation campaigns. We either work alongside your existing scheduler or we help you decide if you should switch. The 3 pillars aren't replacing Jobber — they're the 80% of your business Jobber doesn't touch."

### Objection Pattern

For any objection:
1. **Validate** (don't defend)
2. **Reframe** (flip the cost)
3. **One question** (surface the real thing)

Never stack objections. Handle one, move on.

## BLOCK 10 — Close to Blueprint Call (3 min)

### The Transition
> "Cool. Here's what I want to do next. I'm not going to ask you to make a 9K decision on this call — that'd be weird. What I AM going to do is build you a custom blueprint. I'll take everything from this call, map your current state, design what the installed version looks like in YOUR business specifically, and we'll get on Zoom for 20 minutes. I walk you through it, you ask every question you want, and THEN you decide. No pressure, no hard close. If it's a no after the blueprint, at minimum you walk away with a clearer picture of your own business."

### The Book (offer 2 specific slots)
> "Looking at my calendar — I've got Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am. Which works better?"

If they hesitate:
> "Or first thing next week — Monday 9am?"

If they're still hesitant:
> "What about after you [get home from the trip / finish the job / talk to X]?"

**Never leave it open.** Never say "what works for you?" Always anchor 2 times.

### The Scarcity Frame (use ONLY if real)
> "Quick thing — I'm blocking blueprint slots this week because we're only taking on 4 new builds in April. If you want the blueprint call, let's lock it now while there's still space. If you're a no, that's fine too — I'd rather know now."

### The Confirmation
> "Locked in for [day/time]. I'll send the calendar invite in the next 5 minutes. In the invite I'll include a short video walking you through what to expect on the blueprint call. What email should I send it to?"

### Before You Hang Up
> "One last thing — between now and Thursday, anything else I should know about [their company] when I'm building this out? Any context, constraints, or things you want me to make sure I address?"

Listen. Write it down. This is gold for blueprint prep.

> "Perfect. Talk Thursday at 10."

## Signs of "Yes" vs "No" Mid-Call

Read the room. Don't force a close that isn't there.

### Buying Signals (push forward, lean in)

- They're taking notes or asking you to repeat numbers
- They bring up their own pain without prompting — "yeah that's actually the thing, I was just telling my wife..."
- They ask pricing questions (even objections) — means they're calculating, not rejecting
- They reference a specific number you gave ("so that follow-up leak you said — that's real, I know it is")
- They mention a timeline — "so if we did this, how fast could it be live?"
- They ask about implementation specifics — training, onboarding, who does what
- They bring in other people — "let me grab my partner / spouse / operations lead"
- They go quiet after the cost-of-inaction math. Silence here = math landed.
- They shift from "we" (skeptical distance) to "I" (ownership): "I need to fix this"

### Not-Buying Signals (qualify out, don't push)

- They're multitasking — keyboard sounds, distracted answers, vague
- They answer every discovery question with one-word deflects: "Yeah" / "Uh huh" / "Sure"
- They don't know their numbers and don't care to find out
- They push back on every diagnosis with "we already do that" (defensive, not curious)
- They ask about pricing before you've even finished Sales Ops discovery
- They tell you they're "comparing options" or "just exploring"
- Tone is combative or testing — "prove it to me"
- They interrupt the solution overview with "I don't think I need all that"
- When you ask about revenue or team, they get evasive
- Revenue under $250K (Summit math doesn't work)

### The Qualify-Out (gracefully close when not a fit)

> "Based on what you're telling me, I don't think we're the right fit right now. What you probably need is [specific cheaper thing — HCP, a VA, a marketing course, 12 more months of revenue]. I'd rather be straight with you than take your money and underdeliver. If things change — hit a quarter mil, build a team, feel the bottleneck — call me back. Until then, appreciate the time."

Credibility > commission. Frost would rather have 0 bad clients than 1.

## Blueprint Call Agenda (30-Min Follow-Up)

The blueprint call is where the close happens. Book within 48 hours of the audit.

### Structure

| Block | Min | Goal |
|-------|-----|------|
| 1. Reopen + pulse check | 0–3 | Have anything changed since we talked? |
| 2. Walk the blueprint | 3–18 | Show the current state → installed state visual |
| 3. Timeline + deliverables | 18–22 | What happens in weeks 1-8 |
| 4. Pricing + guarantee restate | 22–25 | Same number, quick confirmation |
| 5. Close | 25–30 | "Does this make sense? Let's start." |

### Opening
> "Good to see you again. Before I walk you through the blueprint — anything shifted since we talked? New info, new questions, anything come up on your end?"

### The Blueprint Walk

Share screen. Show the document (generated via `projects/summit-generator/index.html`).

Structure of what you show:
1. **Their current state** — diagram of where leads come in, where they leak, where ops bottleneck is
2. **The installed state** — same diagram but with Summit's 3 pillars plugged in
3. **Revenue projection** — conservative math based on fixing just the follow-up leak
4. **Timeline** — week-by-week what gets built

Key rule: **Reference their call quotes throughout.** "Remember you told me X — here's the piece that solves that."

### Timeline (show visually)
- Week 1-2: Discovery, CRM setup, pipeline build, website design
- Week 3-4: Website launch, ad creative production, automation sequences live
- Week 5-6: Team SOPs, training rollout, dashboard launch
- Week 7-8: Optimization, coaching, takeover handoff
- Week 9+: Takeover OR rev share partnership

### Pricing Confirmation
> "Same number we discussed — 9K to install everything. Two options after: takeover or rev share. Based on our conversation last week and what you just saw, any questions on that?"

### The Close
> "So — does this make sense for where [company] is right now?"

If yes:
> "Cool. We kick off this week. I'll send the agreement right now, first payment link is in there. Once that's done, I'll invite you to the onboarding doc and we start Monday."

If "I need to think":
> "Totally. One question — is it timing, money, or something else? I'd rather address it now than you sit on it."

### If They Don't Close on the Blueprint Call

Book a 15-min "decision check-in" within 72 hours. Don't let it drift.

> "No pressure. Let's book a quick 15 Friday — you'll have had time to sit with it, maybe talk to whoever you need to talk to, and we can either lock it in or you tell me no. Either way I get to stop thinking about it."

## Key Principles (non-negotiable)

1. **You're diagnosing, not pitching.** Ask questions. Listen. Reflect back in their words.
2. **Use their exact words in diagnosis.** "You said X" hits 10x harder than "I think you have Y."
3. **Frost is the proof.** $300K in 4.5 months with TPS using these exact systems.
4. **Don't sell on this call.** The audit sells the blueprint. The blueprint sells the close.
5. **Be honest.** Not a fit? Say so. Credibility > commission.
6. **Home service specific language.** Seasonality, crews, dispatch, neighborhoods, membership, reviews. Speak their world.
7. **Two specific times.** Never "when works for you?"
8. **Update the CRM in 10 min.** No exceptions. Stale CRM = dead pipeline.
9. **Numbers over adjectives.** "Losing $15K" > "losing a lot."
10. **The silence after the math is the sale.** Don't fill it.
